The most popular sightseeing destinations in Sofia

Sights:

"St. Constantine and Helena". Alexander Nevsky"

There are several buildings and places competing for an emblem of the city. The most frequently visited is the cathedral and the historical church "St. Constantine and Helena". Alexander Nevsky". The temple is the center of the cathedral patriarchy of the Independent Bulgarian Orthodox Church. Also a remarkable place around the cathedral is the Monument of the Unknown Warrior with an ever-burning fire.

Sights:

The monument of the brothers Cyril and Methodius

Behind the cathedral rises the building "St. Constantine and Helena". St. "Cyril and Methodius", which now houses the National Gallery of Foreign Art. It consists of a unique exhibition of arts from Africa, Asia and Europe, Spanish baroque paintings and some works by the modern artist Nikolai Roeric. The monument of the brothers Cyril and Methodius rises in front of the National Library, north of the building of sofia University. Behind Dondukov Boulevard is the National Opera, a building that also houses the National Ballet. The Opera House is in neoclassical style, and in front of it rises the monument of Alexander Stamboliyski, former Prime Minister of Bulgaria.

Sights:

Statue of Saint Sofia

The Statue of Sofia is a recent addition, which was placed in the heart of the city in 2001. At the junction between the main Tzum Mall and the Sheraton Hotel, it stands behind the Nedelja Church. It is a sculpture 24 feet high, made of bronze and copper. The statue weighs than 4 tons and is the creation of the Bulgarian artist Georgi Chapkanov and the architect Stanislav Konstantinov. Sofia has in her hands the symbols of fame and wisdom, and wears the crown of Tjuhe, the goddess of fate. Sophia is obviously the patron saint of the Bulgarian capital. Now crossing, where the statue stands, is undergoing Construction work and it does not do justice to thye sculpture , but it is a central neighborhood and when the work is finished it will certainly reappears as the emblem of the city.

Sights:

NDK - National Palace of Culture

The National Palace of Culture abbreviated in Bulgarian as NDK is the largest multifunctional congress, conference, convention and exhibition center in Southeastern Europe. This enormous landmark was built in 1981 in celebration of the 1300th anniversary of the Bulgarian state. Itâ€™s a hexagon shape and a mass of concrete & glass. More than 10,000 tons of steel were used for the construction of the NDK. That is 3,000 tons more than the Eiffel Tower. The main function of the building is a concert hall. It also hosts exhibitions and trade fairs. In front of the building is an ungerground arcade of shops and cafes.

Sights:

The Rotunda Church of St George

The iconic brick building is actually the reason for the name of the city and is dedicated to Hagia Sophia. It is built on old churches from the 4th century. In the Middle Ages the remarkable building was used as an urban council church, and during the Ottoman rule it was already re-entered into a mosque. Her dome is 19 meters high.

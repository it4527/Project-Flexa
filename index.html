The Capital City Of Bulgaria

Sofia is the capital of the Balkan nation of Bulgaria. It’s in the west of the country, below Vitosha Mountain. It is often re-routed by tourists who head to the coast or ski resorts, but miss something special. Sofia is not a great metropolis, but it is a modern, youthful city, with a scattering of onion-domed churches, Ottoman mosques and stubborn monuments of the Red Army that lend can convince you to hang around and explore further. The capital is full of beautiful parks that you can take a walk and relax in a pleasant air under the foot of vitosha mountain, which is close to the city of Sofia.

Population

Today Sofia is the largest city on the territory of Bulgaria with a total population of 1.67 million people. In recent years, population growth has been recorded due to the high immigration rate in the capital, these numbers are only expected to continue to grow in the years to come.

Coat of arms of the capital of bulgaria

The coat of arms of Bulgaria consists of a crowned golden lion rampant over a dark red shield; above the shield is the Bulgarian historical crown. The shield is supported by two crowned golden lions rampant; below the shield there is compartment in the shape of oak twigs and white bands with the national motto "Unity makes strength" inscribed on them. Description:The current coat of arms of Bulgaria was adopted in 1997. The current arms are a slightly redesigned version of the coat of arms of Bulgaria from the period 1927–1946. Those arms were based on a similar earlier form, firstly used by Tsar Ferdinand I (1887–1918) as his personal ruler's coat of arms.The previous emblem, which combined the traditional gold lion rampant with the pattern of the coat of arms of the Soviet Union, was abandoned since Communist rule ended in the country in 1989.

1900 - First coat of arms of Sofia after the Liberation 1911 - Haralambi Tachev adds the motto "Grows but does not grow old" 1928 - Haralambi Tachev completes the coat of arms with a ribbon with the motto and laurel twigs 1974 - Ivan Radev adds a pentagram and stylizes the coat of arms more strongly 1991 - the coat of arms is restored in its form from the version of Haralambi Tachev from 1928.
